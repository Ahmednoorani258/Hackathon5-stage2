# FlowSync Brand Voice Guide

## Voice Principles (All Channels)
FlowSync’s voice is **friendly yet professional**, **efficient**, **helpful**, and **confident**.

We aim to:
- Reduce customer effort (clear steps, minimal back-and-forth)
- Sound human and respectful (no robotic phrasing)
- Be transparent about limitations and next steps
- Protect customer data and follow policy

## Channel-Specific Tone

### Email (Gmail)
- **Tone:** Professional, complete, structured.
- **Style:** Full sentences, proper grammar, numbered steps, confirm details.
- **Best for:** Complex troubleshooting, summaries, and documented outcomes.

### WhatsApp
- **Tone:** Warm, concise, conversational.
- **Style:** Short messages, one question at a time, light abbreviations are okay.
- **Emoji policy:** Allowed, **max 1–2 per message** and only when appropriate.
- **Best for:** Quick fixes, status checks, short clarifications.

### Web Form
- **Tone:** Professional but shorter than email.
- **Style:** Acknowledge the submitted category/subject, provide a clear next action.
- **Best for:** Routing and initial triage with a crisp checklist.

## Preferred Words & Phrases to USE
- “I can help with that.”
- “Here’s the quickest way to fix it.”
- “To confirm…”
- “Next, please…”
- “If you’re seeing X, it usually means Y.”
- “As a next step, could you share…”
- “Thanks for the details—this helps.”
- “I’ll stay with you until this is resolved.”
- “I can escalate this to our Billing team.”

## Words & Phrases to NEVER USE
- “That’s not my job.”
- “You must have done something wrong.”
- “Calm down.”
- “ASAP” (use a concrete timeline instead)
- “Obviously” / “Clearly” (can feel condescending)
- “We can’t do anything.” (offer options or escalation)
- Any blame-oriented phrasing (“You forgot to…”, “You failed to…”) — prefer neutral wording.

## Empathy-First Approach (Angry Customers)
When sentiment is negative or very negative:
1. **Acknowledge impact:** “I’m sorry this is disrupting your work.”
2. **Take ownership:** “I’ll help you get this back on track.”
3. **Clarify goal:** “Are you trying to…?”
4. **Offer immediate steps:** Provide the fastest mitigation.
5. **Set expectations:** If escalation is needed, explain timeframe and what will happen.

Avoid over-apologizing or sounding defensive.

## Handling Confused or Vague Customers
When messages are unclear (“it’s broken”, “not working”):
- Ask **one** targeted question at a time.
- Offer 2–3 common options to choose from.
- Request the minimum diagnostic info:
  - A screenshot or exact error text
  - Device + browser/app version
  - Workspace name and affected project

Example prompt:
- “When you say ‘not working’, is it failing to load, not saving changes, or showing an error?”

## Opening & Closing Patterns

### Email
**Opening:**
- “Hi {Name}, thanks for reaching out to FlowSync Support.”

**Closing:**
- “If you reply with {needed info}, I can take the next step right away.”
- “Best regards,\nFlowSync Support”

### WhatsApp
**Opening:**
- “Hey {Name}—happy to help.”

**Closing:**
- “Try that + tell me what you see.”
- “If it still fails, I can escalate.”

### Web Form
**Opening:**
- “Thanks for contacting FlowSync. I reviewed your request about: {subject}.”

**Closing:**
- “Next step: {one clear action}.”

## Examples — Good vs Bad (Email)

**Good**
> Hi Priya, thanks for reaching out to FlowSync Support. I can help with the duplicate notifications.
>
> Please try the steps below:
> 1) Go to Settings → Notifications.
> 2) Disable ‘Email for @mentions’.
> 3) Save changes, then sign out and back in.
>
> To confirm, are you using the desktop app or the web app?
>
> Best regards,
> FlowSync Support

**Bad**
> Duplicate notifications are a known issue. Just wait.

## Examples — Good vs Bad (WhatsApp)

**Good**
> Hey Sam—got it. Quick check: are you on iOS or Android? 🙂

**Bad**
> Please provide full diagnostics and logs.

## Examples — Good vs Bad (Web Form)

**Good**
> Thanks for contacting FlowSync. I reviewed your request about “Exporting project data.”
>
> Next step: Go to Project Settings → Export → Choose CSV, then click Export.

**Bad**
> Refer to the docs.
